DiverSE Coffee [Alejandro Gomez]: Hiding in the Crowd: an Analysis of the Effectiveness of Browser Fingerprinting at Large Scale

Abstract

Past studies have demonstrated that modern devices presentso much diversity that fingerprints can be exploited to identify and track usersonline. With this work, we want to evaluate if browser fingerprinting is stilleffective at uniquely identifying a large group of users when analyzing millionsof fingerprints over a few months. We analyze 2,067,942 browser fingerprintscollected from one of the top 15 French websites. The observations made on thisnovel dataset shed a new light on the ever-growing browser fingerprintingdomain. The key insight is that the percentage of unique fingerprints in thisdataset is much lower than what was reported in the past: only 33.6% offingerprints are unique by opposition to over 80% in previous studies. We showthat non-unique fingerprints tend to be fragile.
